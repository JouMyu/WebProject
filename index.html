Team Summary

Aston Martin Red Bull Racing is a Formula One racing team first racing in 2005. The cars race with a Honda produced
V6 Hybrid Engines. The team currently races with an Austrian License, although did race under an English license from 2005 to 2006.
The team is one of two, owned by Red Bull GmbH. The other is named Scuderia AlphaTauri (previously, Scuderia Toro Rosso),
made to promote Red Bull's clothing line. Red Bull Racing is the main team whilst Scuderia AlphaTauri is often seen
as the feeder team which provides a route into the main competitive team.

Team Manager

The current team manager is Christian Horner who has been managing since the founding of Aston Martin Red Bull Racing.
Christian Horner has helped Red Bull Racing 8 world titles, (4 Constructors' Championships, 4 Drivers' Championships).
Before becoming a manager, he raced in Formula 3000 until age 25, when he felt he wasn't as fast as the other drivers on the grid
and subsequently retired from racing.

Engine Supplier/s

The team's current engine supplier is Honda who has been supplying the team since the 2019 season.

When the team was founded, the engine used was from Cosworth, a British engineering company, which for a short while, supplied engines to various teams
such as Lotus and Mclaren.

In 2006 the engine supplier was from a rival team, Ferrari. This quickly changed in 2007 when Red Bull decided to switch to Renault engines.
However, after an uneventful 2015 season placing them in the lowest position in 7 years, Red Bull forced a rebranding of Renault's engines and
ran with Renault engines rebranded as TAG-Heuer. Another upset in 2018 caused Red Bull to terminate the partnership with Renault, instead opting for a Honda engine in 2019. Ironically, the Red Bull driver Daniel Ricciardo moved to race for Renault in the 2019 season on a 2 year contract until 2021
